About Indigenous Filipinos

The Philippines is home to over 110 indigenous ethnolinguistic groups, comprising about 15% of the total population. These communities have preserved distinct cultures, traditions, and knowledge systems for thousands of years.


Collectively known as Katutubo, they inhabit the mountainous regions, forests, and coastal areas across the archipelago. Each group possesses unique languages, spiritual beliefs, arts, and sustainable ways of living in harmony with nature.


Despite facing challenges such as displacement and cultural erosion, indigenous Filipinos continue to uphold their ancestral heritage while contributing to the nation's cultural diversity.

Major Indigenous Tribes

Cultural Heritage

Traditional Crafts

Intricate weaving, pottery, wood carving, and jewelry making using sustainable materials and ancient techniques.

Music & Dance

Unique musical instruments, chants, and dances that tell stories of nature, ancestors, and daily life.

Sustainable Living

Traditional ecological knowledge for sustainable agriculture, forest management, and natural resource conservation.

Oral Traditions

Rich oral literature including epics, myths, legends, and proverbs passed down through generations.

How You Can Support

Indigenous communities face challenges including land rights issues, cultural erosion, and economic marginalization. Here's how you can help:

Ethical Tourism

Visit indigenous communities responsibly, respecting their culture and traditions while supporting their livelihoods.

Buy Authentic Crafts

Purchase directly from indigenous artisans to support their traditional crafts and ensure fair compensation.

Advocate & Educate

Raise awareness about indigenous rights and issues. Share accurate information and challenge stereotypes.
